Meet Norbeth & Todd
Your Didgeridoo Instructors

Norbeth Granado, born in San Jose, Costa Rica has played and taught didgeridoo, Native American flute, native drums and other indigenous instruments for more than a decade. A descendant of Central American native healers and shamans, Norbeth was less than a year old when he began receiving training in a variety of spiritual traditions. Among those spiritual and musical traditions are Central and Southern American, Lakota, Choctaw, Huichol, and even include a course with His Holiness the 14th Dalai Lama, Tenzin Gyatso. He has certified in several healing modalities, Reiki (level lll Master), Zenith light Practitioner, Breathwork, Facilitator and various body-working techniques. Norbeth has held countless drum circles, healing circles, teaching circles and hosted various indigenous ceremonies. He is currently finishing his course work from the Institute of Transpersonal Psychology (ITP) in Palo Alto , Ca. to certify him as a life coach.

Todd Zimmerman is a Musician, Transformational Life Coach / Mentor , Hand Analyst, Coach Trainer, and Breathwork Facilitator. He and Dr. Rosie Kuhn of The Paradigm Shifts Coaching Group (www.theparadigmshifts.com) have provided Coach training programs at The Institute of Transpersonal Psychology (ITP) for seven years. Todd began facilitating indigenous and mainstream trainings, transformational modalities and workshops in 1986. His love affair with Drums, Didgeridoo and Native American Flute has blessed his life ever since. He has been playing the Acoustic Guitar since High School. Todd and his life long best friend Daryl Lowery have been drumming with the Developmentally Disabled community for just under two years and have recently launched a new business and website (www.rhythmicfoundation.com) as a way to better serve the community and to grow our passion.
